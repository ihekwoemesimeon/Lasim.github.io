From Mini-Tournament to Global Powerhouse

The FIFA Club World Cup has undergone a revolutionary transformation in 2025, growing from a niche tournament into one of the biggest spectacles in global football. With 32 of the world’s best clubs battling it out in the United States, this expanded edition mirrors the traditional FIFA World Cup format and marks a major shift in the football landscape.

From Mini-Tournament to Global Powerhouse

Previously an annual, seven-team event held each December, the FIFA Club World Cup often flew under the radar. But FIFA's 2025 overhaul has changed everything. Held every four years, this new format brings together 32 clubs from across all six footballing confederations, showcasing champions and top performers from Europe, South America, Africa, Asia, and beyond. According to FIFA, the aim is to “create a global club competition that matches the prestige of the national team World Cup.”
